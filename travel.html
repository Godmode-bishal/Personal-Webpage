Chennai Detroit of Asia

A city where everyone is welcome. I have never seen any city with the diversity Chennai possesses. Add the fact that it's one of the classiest cities of India with it's potpourri of cultures, extravagant temples, ancient churches, monuments and beaches. Plus, it's also the home of Carnatic music system.

Kolkata City of Joy

Aha !! The Begalis !! No state can beat the contributions of this state in Science, Arts, Literature and culture. Though things have declined after the 1980s, the place is still worth a visit. The city is like a faded watercolor painting. The old houses still live. The Victorian art and architecture still lives. The yellow cabs and the trams still live. My personal opinion is that in an attempt to hold on the glory of the past, the state has failed to modernize.

Karaikal Indian and French alike

A smooth 6 hours road journey from Chennai takes you to Karaikal, a major city of Puducherry. I was surprised to find people who could speak French and had dual Indo-French citizenship. The entire city is filled with perpendicular streets, which is not very common in India. The place is very clean and the people are very friendly. Unfortunately, I don't have pictures of this place as I lost all of them a while back. Strongly recommended for people looking to spend a quiet weekend.

Mumbai Hollywood of India

The best place I have ever been to. The culture, glamour and gully cricket were the best things for me. With its lovely chaotic streets, surprising contrasts, impressive colonial buildings, colorful atmosphere, nightlife, Mumbai is THE place to be. As long as you don't call Mumbai "Bombay" and know Marathi, you should be very comfortable there. Unfortunately, I don't have pictures of this place as I lost all of them a while back. Recommended for fun freaks.

Chandkheda near the Boston of India

Has to be one of the most diverse places I have visited. It's near to Ahmedabad, famous for its winter carnivals, garba, and cuisine. Unfortunately, I don't have pictures of this place as I lost all of them a while back.

Ooty Queen of Hill Stations

It's an unlikely place as you don't really expect to find a cold place in a hot state. The view, the weather, the gardens and the lakes are out of this world. Though the lack of people gives this place an eerie feeling. Unfortunately, I don't have pictures of this place as I lost all of them a while back.

Mysore City of Palaces

A place of interest for art and architecture lovers. The palaces are amazing and surprisingly scientific. I have heard that Mysore attracts the same number of tourists as the Taj. The Mysore palace is decorated beautifully in the months of September and October to celebrate some festival,and it's view from the Brindavan gardens is a treat to watch. Apart from these, Tipu Sultan's palace, Butterfly park and Chamundi hills are a must visit. Unfortunately, I don't have pictures of this place as I lost all of them a while back.

Delhi City of Rallies

Delhi is not a very big place. But, it's a place where you can find anything. New Delhi is the national capital of India and hence, almost all the strategically important offices are here. Delhi had been an important for all the previous dynaties that ruled over India, and hence a lot of monuments and World heritage sites can be found here. People of Delhi are street smart and intelligent. Apart from the pollution, everthing else in Delhi is just fine.

Agra City of Taj

The city of the Taj. That says it all. I have visited many monuments, but trust me, nothing beats the Taj. The beauty of the white marble combined with the history and its serenity makes it one of a kind. No wonder it's one of the seven wonders. Apart from the Taj Mahal, you can also visit the Agra fort and try out the sweets of Agra.

Agartala Hometown !!

Small little city in one edge of the North East. Underrated, but not under derveloped. Famous for it's temples, tribes and culture. Unfortunately, I don't have pictures of this place as I thought of clicking any pictures here. My bad.

Guwahati Gateway of North East India

A beautiful place with beautiful people. Bihu, one-horned rhinoceros, the brahmaputra, the historic places of the Ahom kingdom are some of it's specialities. Unfortunately, I don't have pictures of this place as I lost all of them a while back.

Andaman and Nicobar Islands Unique Islands

This place is different from all the other places. Isolated, it has a lot of different tribes, out of which some are hostile and some are not. Consists of many island, out of which only a few have people in them. Most of the island are closed for visitors. You can go into some after getting special permission. I felt very small there, given the size of the sea.
