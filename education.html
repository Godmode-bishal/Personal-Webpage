An uniform energy distribution model for Wireless Sensor networks.

This paper proposes a model that deals with the problem of determining optimal path for routing a group of wireless server through a forest of immobile wireless network clients divided into uniform clusters or cells, and embedded in a plane, such that the average energy dissipation of the wireless network clients is uniform. From uniformity of energy dissipation, we mean that the energy dissipated by all the wireless network clients after R rounds of traversal is similar, where R>1. This ensures that we will be able to collect the data from all parts of the WSN for a period of time, without losing any data. This will result to a better aggregation of data and consequently give better processing results. We delve upon the problem of minimizing the traversal cost of wireless network server, subject to constraints in the travel path function and the distance formulation. We also deal with the situation of selecting a path to minimize of interference of wireless network servers with each other.
